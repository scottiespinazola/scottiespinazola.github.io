The Chris Paul Effect

2021-04-20

There is no I in the word team but once or twice or honestly however many you want to say in every generation a player makes you think that maybe it does just come down to one person.

Who comes to mind for me is Michael Jordan, Lebron James, Tom Brady, and you can even put Mike Trout in there for making the angels a pretty solid team.

But those players are great and all but they take the spotlight away from players that deserve more.

Who I am taking about is Chris Paul, Point Guard (God), for the Phoneix Suns.

Chris Paul has had a career that most would say is Hall of Fame, just doesn’t have a ring but its coming, and one that shows that a player can change a team for good but once that players levels the team will be forced to show its true colors.

First data we are going to just be looking at is his journey, in his 16 year carer Paul has played for 5 different teams and shows through his numbers that he has only gotten better throughout.

As seen above in the circular chart when looking at just wins and nothing else the years were Paul is not with the Hornets they are not so good at winning basketball games, But the years that they have Paul they for some reason (being sarcastic) seem to win games.

Paul in just around three seasons leads them to over 50 wins. A lot goes into a team, of course, but when the data is laid out in front of you it seems to me that there is a specific key factor that the Hornets needed to win games.

Does this not seems to be swaying you enough?

Alright well lets keep going then, With Paul being a Point Guard he is going to have the ball in his hands a lot (controlling the pace and starting the play are just somethings) and that will reflect on how well the offense will run.

Now being even more specific the offense only will run well if you can shoot the ball, so an important factor that we can look at will be the field goal percentage.

So what we will be looking at will be the wins and field goal percentage over the years and so if there is a correlation through a p-value and a chart.

winlosslogs <- hornets_before_during_after %>% mutate(winloss = case_when(
  grepl("W", Result) ~ 1, 
  grepl("L", Result) ~ 0)
)
hornetshooting <- winlosslogs %>% 
  group_by(Year) %>% 
  summarise(
    Wins = sum(winloss),

hornet78 <- hornetshooting %>% filter(Year == "2007-2008")

hornet45 <- hornetshooting %>% filter(Year == "2004-2005")

ggplot() +

  geom_point(data=hornet45, aes(x=avgFGPCT, y=Wins), color="red") +
  geom_point(data=hornet78, aes(x=avgFGPCT, y=Wins), color="green") +

But obviously when looking at the years the Hornets will be bad coming in seeng that they got Paul in the draft. But the second lowest dot on the chart is the year after Paul leaves.
