The Chris Paul Effect

2021-04-20

There is no I in the word team but once or twice or honestly however many you want to say in every generation a player makes you think that maybe it does just come down to one person.

Who comes to mind for me is Michael Jordan, Lebron James, Tom Brady, and you can even put Mike Trout in there for making the angels a pretty solid team.

But those players are great and all but they take the spotlight away from players that deserve more.

Who I am taking about is Chris Paul, Point Guard (God), for the Phoneix Suns.

Chris Paul has had a career that most would say is Hall of Fame, just doesn’t have a ring but its coming, and one that shows that a player can change a team for good but once that players levels the team will be forced to show its true colors.

First data we are going to just be looking at is his journey, in his 16 year carer Paul has played for 5 different teams and shows through his numbers that he has only gotten better throughout.

In his 16th season he is putting up numbers to break records and when we look at the Suns later he might also finally reach the peak by ending the year with a ring.

But lets not get to ahead of ourselves, Paul’s career and this article really need to be brought back to his beginning with the New Orleans/Oklahoma City Hornets.

These data of over the years start with the 2004-05 season which was the year before, go through his time with the Hornets, and ends with the year after Paul leaves town.

Just to dip your guys toes into what is going to be talked about lets settle in with a basic look at the wins through the specific years that we are going to talking about with the Hornets.

winlosshornets <- hornets_before_during_after %>% mutate(winloss = case_when(
  grepl("W", Result) ~ 1, 
  grepl("L", Result) ~ 0)
)
years <- winlosshornets %>% 
  group_by(Year, Opp) %>% 
  summarize(wins = sum(winloss))
## `summarise()` has grouped output by 'Year'. You can override using the `.groups` argument.
ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=years, aes(x=Year, weight=wins, fill=Opp)) + 
  theme_minimal()+ 
  coord_polar() +
  labs(y = "Wins", title = "Chris Paul Equals Wins", subtitle = "Foreshadowing to what is to come", caption = "Source: Basketball Reference | By: Scottie Spinazola")

As seen above in the circular chart when looking at just wins and nothing else the years were Paul is not with the Hornets they are not so good at winning basketball games, But the years that they have Paul they for some reason (being sarcastic) seem to win games.

Paul in just around three seasons leads them to over 50 wins. A lot goes into a team, of course, but when the data is laid out infront of you it seeems to me that there is a specific key factor that the Hornets needed to win games.
