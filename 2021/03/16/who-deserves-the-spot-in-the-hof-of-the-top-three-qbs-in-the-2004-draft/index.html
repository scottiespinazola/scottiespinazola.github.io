Who Deserves the Spot in the HOF of the Top Three QBs in the 2004 Draft

2021-03-16

As a Sports fan I do not intend on making this into something that will make people mad or to bring down a players career by nit picking through there career.

This is just intended to see that with three careers in my generation that are close and honestly to me seem pretty interesting to see who stat wise would be inducted in canton at the Pro Football Hall of fame.

The three quarterbacks I will be discussing are three that can be described as being in the weirdest draft day where two were traded for eachother in Eli Manning and Philip Rivers and then the other cooming from a non power five school but showed that doesn’t matter much in Ben Roethlisberger.

As we take this adventure and look throughout their careers I will be refering to them by different names; Rivers for Philip Rivers, Manning for Eli Manning and Big Ben for Ben Roethlisberger hope there is no confusion.

When first looking at the draft of 2004 it could have been a lot different because of the way that the first pick ended up.

Eli manning was the top Quarterback coming out of Ole Miss but did not want to be drafted number one overall by the San Diego Chargers because the market would not fit a Manning, so there next target was Philip Rivers from North Carolina State at Four. That landed Eli with the New York Giants and Rivers to the Chargers.

That is all of the drama that was going on in the top ten of the draft but right outside of the that was 11th overall pick Big Ben Roethlisberger from Miami of Ohio.

So now they have their destinations and the routes that they took and the only one that changed teams since the draft drama and after was Rivers when he went to the Colts, playoff run but not past the first round.

The three of them when looking at the stats and just being able to watch them live when I was able to it is pretty clear that the three are not know for being the most mobile quarterbacks; but hey all 18 Pro Bowls and four Super Bowl rings make up this group.

Rivers takes the yardage test but not by much, I would say that this came at a surprise but not because of Rivers. Eli comes in at last, which surprised me, maybe the Manning name, but what he has done with his career is something that I guess doesn’t show up on his stats.

Rivers tops both because of what he has done with his side arm type passes, Big Ben does take the crown for the rushing yards but that is because of his x-factor being escape artist (for the Madden fans).

All of these stats do make sense though when you take them into consideration, the teams that all three played for always had those quick little running backs that could take the screens or dumpdowns that some mobile quarterbacks would take for themselves.

Chargers have had Austin Ekeler, Steelers Le’von Bell and the Giants with Saquon Barkley, Amid Bradshaw,and Teke Barbor. Helping pad those passing stats make sense of the crazy lopsided story being told in the chart above but also it shows the story is that Rivers did more with his completetions than Big Ben or Eli.

Yards can be dinks and dunks from screens or under arms passes as we see from Mahomes but Touchdowns and Int do come from good passes and smart/dumb descision making. Or you could be Jamis Winston and be throwing the 30 touchdowns in a season, lead the league in passing but also lead the league in interceptions by a landslide. But with us and these players we are not going to be talking about players that make head shaking mistakes.

Obviously Super Bowl rings are impressive but let me ask you this… If Rivers had just one ring would that push him into the clear spot taker? Now Rivers has better career yards, more touchdowns, only a few more interceptions yet gets all of the slander when put up against his fellow draft mates.

But when wrapping this thing in a pretty little bow that it deserves would be this, the question was asked “Who would take the spot in canton if there was only one spot left?” and I hate to say it because I was pulling for Rivers but it was said before Big Ben Roethlisberger gets the spot.
